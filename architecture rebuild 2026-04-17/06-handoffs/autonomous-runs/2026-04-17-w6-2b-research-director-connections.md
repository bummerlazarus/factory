## 7. Cross-silo verification

```sql
SELECT count(*) AS total,
       count(*) FILTER (WHERE metadata->>'a_source' <> metadata->>'b_source') AS cross_silo_count,
       count(*) FILTER (WHERE metadata->>'a_source' = metadata->>'b_source') AS same_source_count
FROM public.reference_docs WHERE kind='connection';
```

→ `{total: 10, cross_silo_count: 10, same_source_count: 0}`

**10/10 rows are cross-silo.** Acceptance criterion 3 satisfied.

All 10 pairs landed on `csv <-> markdown` (the two smallest corpora
also happen to contain the most near-duplicate records — Edmund's
parish-ministry benchmarking tables live in both formats). Higher
similarity_threshold or a larger corpus with better-distributed
embeddings would produce more varied pairs; that's a tuning/content
problem, not a bug. The algorithm is correctly picking the 10 highest-
similarity cross-source pairs in the window.
