The Problem

Your knowledge is stored.
It's not synthesized.

Every creator has the same problem. Ten years of notes in Notion. A folder of voice memos on your phone. A stack of Kindle highlights. A couple hundred YouTube transcripts you've studied. A brand bible you wrote once and haven't opened in six months.

Somewhere in that pile is your actual thinking — the frameworks you've coined, the pillar quotes you keep reaching for, the positioning lines that are genuinely yours. But when you sit down to write the next thing, you start from scratch anyway.

How it works

Three stages.
Capture, scan, canonize.

Stage 01

Capture

Everything you say or save lands in one database. Each piece gets converted into a vector — a mathematical fingerprint of meaning — so the system knows what's close to what, regardless of folder or tag.

  • iPhone Shortcut + voice memo
  • URL bookmarklet
  • Notion database sync
  • YouTube + PDF + file upload
  • Agent chat captures
Stage 02

Scan

Every night and every week a small team of agents goes to work on the pile. They cluster, contradict, research, draft, and gate — each with a defined job.

  • Semantic clustering nightly
  • Cross-silo connection scan
  • Contradiction detection
  • Outbound research fetch
  • Permanent-gate consensus review
Stage 03

Canonical IP

Surviving drafts become canon. A compressed essay in your voice, naming your references, quoting your pillar lines, showing how your frameworks connect. Living — as new captures land, the doc refreshes.

  • Your voice, your references
  • Versioned + editable
  • Embedded back into memory
  • Read by every agent you run
  • The substrate for content

What it unlocks

Five things that don't happen
with a second brain alone.

You stop forgetting what you think

The framework you had in March is still here in November — refined, connected to the three books you read in between.

You stop restarting from scratch

You update the canonical doc. You don't rebuild it. Every new capture strengthens the canon instead of scattering it.

Your agents become multipliers

Content, outreach, drafts, decks — all pulled from canon, not generic LLM vibes. Volume goes up. Voice stays true.

Cross-silo creativity becomes automatic

The Von Hildebrand × Will Storr × Julia Galef connection you couldn't have stitched by hand — the engine finds it and names it for you.

You get an IP map

A living snapshot of what you've authored, what you've hinted at, what your audience is asking for, and where the gaps are.

The system compounds with use

More capture → tighter themes → sharper skills → more varied drafts → better metrics → more time to capture. Exponential, not linear.
